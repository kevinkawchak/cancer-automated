# IEC 80601-2-77:2019

- Designation: IEC 80601-2-77:2019
- Issuing body: International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)
- Title: Medical Electrical Equipment, Particular Requirements for the Basic
  Safety and Essential Performance of Robotically Assisted Surgical Equipment

## Scope

IEC 80601-2-77 is the particular standard for robotically assisted surgical
equipment (RASD). It is read together with the general standard IEC 60601-1. It
defines basic safety and essential performance requirements specific to surgical
robots, including hazardous-situation detection, emergency stopping, and limits
on hazardous output.

## Clauses VVUQ-02 uses

- Essential performance: the robot must maintain commanded tip position within a
  declared accuracy or transition to a safe state. VVUQ-02 declares a 0.05 mm RMS
  fingertip positioning target validated against an optical-tracker reference
  (gate 01).
- Hazardous output limiting: applied tip force must be limited. VVUQ-02 enforces a
  per-fingertip soft cap of 2.5 N and hard cap of 3.0 N (gate 02), and a bimanual
  cumulative soft cap of 5.0 N and hard cap of 6.0 N.
- Emergency stop and hazardous-situation response: VVUQ-02 enforces a 3 ms hand
  and arm e-stop budget and a 50 microsecond joint park budget, with a 10
  microsecond one-tick proximity hard stop near vessels (gates 06 and 10).

## How VVUQ-02 applies it

Gates 01, 02, 05, 06, 07, and 10 carry IEC 80601-2-77 derived limits. The 3.0 N
fingertip hard cap is set well below the general RASD tip-force ceiling, and the
3 ms e-stop is set well below the general e-stop requirement, to preserve margin.
