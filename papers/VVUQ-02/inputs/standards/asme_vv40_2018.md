# ASME V&V 40-2018

- Designation: ASME V&V 40-2018
- Issuing body: American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME)
- Title: Assessing Credibility of Computational Modeling through Verification and
  Validation, Application to Medical Devices

## Scope

ASME V&V 40-2018 provides a risk-informed framework for establishing how much
verification and validation evidence a computational model needs in order to be
credible for a specific use. It is the standard referenced by the United States
FDA 2023 guidance on assessing the credibility of computational modeling and
simulation in medical device submissions.

## Constructs VVUQ-02 uses

1. Question of interest: the specific decision the model informs.
2. Context of use (COU): the role and scope of the model in informing that
   decision.
3. Model risk = model influence x decision consequence.
4. Credibility goals are set in proportion to model risk.
5. Verification and validation activities are sized to meet the credibility goals.

## How VVUQ-02 applies it

- Each of the 10 VVUQ gates declares a question of interest and a context of use
  in `codegen/docs/vvuq_gate_spec.md`.
- Model risk drives gate strictness in `codegen/config/vvuq_thresholds.yaml`. The
  three immediate-catastrophe gates (06 vascular no-fly, 09 human collision, 10
  fault and e-stop) carry the highest model risk and therefore the highest
  credibility goals (verification pass fraction 1.0, lowest coefficient-of-
  variation bounds, and an extra hard predicate).
- The validation dimension compares observed metrics against an independent
  reference in `codegen/data/reference/`, consistent with V&V 40 section 9.
