# ISO/TS 15066:2016

- Designation: ISO/TS 15066:2016
- Issuing body: International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
- Title: Robots and Robotic Devices, Collaborative Robots, Biomechanical Limits

## Scope

ISO/TS 15066 supplements ISO 10218-1 and ISO 10218-2 for collaborative robot
operation. It provides data-driven biomechanical limits for contact between a
robot and a human, including power-and-force-limiting thresholds for transient and
quasi-static contact across body regions.

## Clauses VVUQ-02 uses

- Power and force limiting: contact forces and pressures must remain below the
  biomechanical limits for the relevant body region.
- Speed and separation monitoring: the robot must maintain a protective
  separation distance from a human and reduce speed or stop as the distance
  shrinks.

## How VVUQ-02 applies it

Gate 09 (shared-or-human-collision) implements a whole-body human-aware finite
state machine with bands clear, proximity, contact, and unsafe, keyed on the
minimum distance from any humanoid link to any human actor in
`codegen/config/shared_or_actors.yaml`. The gate requires the minimum human
clearance never to drop below a hard floor and the intrusion-reaction latency to
stay within budget. Gate 02 force limiting reuses the same power-and-force-
limiting principle at the fingertip.
