# ISO 13482:2014

- Designation: ISO 13482:2014
- Issuing body: International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
- Title: Robots and Robotic Devices, Safety Requirements for Personal Care Robots

## Scope

ISO 13482 specifies safety requirements for personal care robots, including
mobile servant robots and restraint-type and person-carrier robots. It is the
service-robot stability and fall-prevention standard most applicable to a
free-standing bipedal humanoid that is not a fixed-base industrial manipulator.

## Clauses VVUQ-02 uses

- Stability and fall prevention: the robot must maintain stability under expected
  loads and disturbances and prevent hazardous falls.
- Hazards due to robot motion and contact: motion must not create hazardous
  contact with persons.

## How VVUQ-02 applies it

Gate 03 (whole-body-balance) enforces a zero-moment-point (ZMP) stability margin
that must remain at or above an 8 mm floor inside the support polygon while the
humanoid exerts surgical reaction forces. The balance model in
`codegen/config/balance_model.yaml` defines mass, centre of mass, support polygon
geometry, and the disturbance budget. A standing humanoid can fall over where a
fixed coelomic arm cannot, which is why this gate exists and the 8-arm baseline
has no equivalent.
