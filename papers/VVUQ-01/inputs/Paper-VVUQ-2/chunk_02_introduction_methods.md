## 1. Introduction

The May 2022 Madabushi et al. "Review: Role of Model-Informed Drug Development Approaches in the Lifecycle of Drug Development and Regulatory Decision-Making" provides requirements to addressing regulatory burden that were utilized in the current study [03IntroMadabushi]. The earliest application of MIDD to inform FDA regulatory decisions began in the 1990s. FDA in vitro in vivo correlation (IVIVC) modeling was then finalized in 1997 to reduce burden by reducing tests in human subjects with analogous time or cost savings. The Agency's efforts for advancing MIDD "received a big boost with the recognition of MIDD as one of the regulatory decision tools to support drug development and review" under the prescription drug user fee act (PDUFA VI) [04IntroCraig, 05IntroGalluppi]. However, software disadvantages exist, such as "the need for the user to know how to manually code in the native computational language of the platform, black box approaches to some software defined equations, limitations in biopharmaceutics variable selection, or even availability of the software itself [03IntroMadabushi, 04IntroCraig]."

The November 2023 FDA "Assessing the Credibility of Computational Modeling and Simulation in Medical Device Submissions" applies the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) V&V 40 Assessing Credibility of Computational Modeling standard regarding assessing verification, validation, and uncertainty quantification (VVUQ). The standard utilizes established bench testing as a framework for less controllable clinical studies for demonstrating computational modeling and simulation (CM&S) credibility. This guidance contains a multi-step table that details the connections between ASME V&V 40–2018 with FDA guidance sections [01IntroFDA2023].

Additional FDA guidance through the November 2024 "M15 General Principles for Model-Informed Drug Development" further detailed how to assess model-informed drug development (MIDD). Instructions regarding verification testing procedures, installation, and version tracking; as well as validation for potential bias assisted in formalizing new approaches in drug development. Appendix 1 includes instructions for using Question of Interest, Context of Use, and Model Impact. The MIDD planning stage provides additional instruction regarding "Appropriateness of Proposed MIDD" and "Technical Criteria" leading up to "Outcome of the MIDD Evidence Assessment [02IntroFDA2025]."

## 2. Methods

Performant conversational AI models were used in this study to address software disadvantages with text protocols supporting code, biopharmaceutical variables addressed by imported settings from a prior QSP study [20KawchakQSPPDAC], and ease of use english conversations throughout the project with state-of-the-art AI. AI models were further used in this study to process the FDA Assessing the Credibility and M15 documents to produce test recommendations executed by the user throughout the study [01IntroFDA2023]. Additionally, this current study's abstract utilized Appendix 1 elements of MIDD and ASME V&V 40 [02IntroFDA2025, 01IntroFDA2023] in accordance with FDA compliance.

Digital twin arms were based on a prior paper's Daraxonrasib + Mitazalimab + liposomal Irinotecan combinations with Control nal-IRI+5FU control [20KawchakQSPPDAC, 19KawchakSimPDAC, 18KawchakPDAC]; and additional arms of Magrolimab and MRTX-1133 with Gemcitabine + nab-Paclitaxel control were from the most recent Quantitative Systems Pharmacology (QSP) study.
