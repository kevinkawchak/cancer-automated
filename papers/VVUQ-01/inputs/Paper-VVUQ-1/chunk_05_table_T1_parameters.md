## Table T1 — Key QSP Model Parameters and Assumptions

| Parameter | Baseline | Details | Notes |
|-----------|----------|---------|-------|
| **r_s** (Tumor sensitive growth rate) | ~0.0115 per day (varied 0.008–0.014) | Net growth rate for sensitive tumor cells; set for ~60 day doubling time (mid-range PDAC). | Adjusted so untreated tumors reach lethal size in ~6–12 mo. Varied ±20% across patients. |
| **r_r** (Tumor resistant growth rate) | Same order as r_s (~0.011 per day) | Growth rate for resistant subclone. Assumed equal or slightly lower than r_s (no major fitness cost for resistance). | Sensitivity: halving r_r had minor effect on outcomes (resistant cells growing slower slightly delays relapse). |
| **f_res** (Initial resistant fraction) | 5% (Beta distribution, most 0–10%) | Fraction of tumor volume inherently drug-resistant at baseline. High-impact parameter: increasing to 15% or 30% lowers efficacy. | Represents intratumoral heterogeneity; not evolved during treatment in this version. |
| K (Carrying capacity) | ~4 × initial tumor volume (approx.) | Effective carrying capacity for logistic growth. Set large enough that most virtual patients die/progress before reaching K. | Mainly prevents numerical blow-up. Could be interpreted as max tumor burden compatible with life. |
| **k_chemo** (Chemo kill rate) | Calibrated to Arm A efficacy | Effect of gemcitabine + nab-paclitaxel on tumor kill. Profiled as periodic spikes (weekly dosing); tuned so that in HA_low patients ~23% ORR, ~8–10 mo OS (Arm A baseline). | Scaled by compartment drug concentration (lower in poorly perfused regions). In HA_high patients, effective k_chemo is lower (poor penetration) unless PEGPH20 is given. |
| PEGPH20 effect (Stroma modifier) | +30% to chemo penetration in HA_high | Pegvorhyaluronidase temporarily increases drug penetration in high-stroma tumors. Modeled as a 1.3× multiplier on local [chemo] for HA_high patients. | Chosen such that Arm D shows higher ORR (due to initially better drug delivery) but no OS gain without other factors (matching HALO-301 outcome). |
| **k_target** (KRAS inhibitor kill) | E_max = 0.072 per day (base); EC50 = 1 µM (est.) | Effect of KRAS G12D inhibitor (Arm C) or KRAS G12C inhibitor (Arms H/I) on tumor. This is an added death rate for mutant cells. E_max set so that at high drug levels, tumor growth halts or reverses (near-saturation). | Sensitivity: 0.5 or 1.5× increasing E_max changed Arm C ORR by only a few points (70% → ~66% or 72%), indicating baseline is on a plateau of dose-response (robust). |
| k_immune (Immune kill rate, base) | ~1×10^−4 per day (very low) | Baseline tumor kill by the immune system without immunotherapy. Set near zero to reflect PDAC's evasive nature. | Essentially 0% ORR in absence of treatment (Arm G or PD-1 monotherapy yields ~0% ORR, as expected). |
| PD-1 inhibitor effect | 3–5× increase in k_immune | Pembrolizumab (Arms E, H) effect modeled as a multiplier on immune kill rate. Chosen so PD-1 monotherapy still results in ~0% ORR. | Ensured Arm H (chemo + PD-1 + CD40) outperforms chemo alone in KRAS G12C patients. |
| CD40 agonist effect | + add 3–5× to k_immune | Mitazalimab (Arms E, H, I) provides an immune "push." Stacked multiplicatively with PD-1: in Arm I (KRAS G12C + CD40, no chemo) it increases k_immune moderately; in Arm H (with PD-1) together create a boost. | Parameter tuned so Arm I (KRAS G12C + CD40, no chemo) achieves 58% ORR, indicating meaningful tumor control, and Arm H (adding chemo) reaches ~62% ORR and the longest OS. |
| CD47 inhibitor effect | + macrophage kill term (minor) | Magrolimab (Arm B) allows macrophages to phagocytose tumor cells. Implemented as a small constant kill term or slight increase to k_immune (since the immune term includes all immune-mediated kill). | Calibrated such that Arm B showed a higher ORR than Arm A (model ~50.5% vs 50.0%) and a slight improvement in PFS, but essentially the same OS (~10.1 vs 10.3 mo); which reflects a short-term benefit. |
| Toxicity thresholds | (drop out if toxicity score > X) | Each drug contributes to a "toxicity score" (chemo heavily, PD-1 moderately, CD40 heavily, CD47 mildly). Grade ≥3 AE probability was calibrated so that multi-agent arms reach >90% incidence (Arm A ~99% Grade ≥3, reflecting FOLFIRINOX toxicity), while less intense arms have lower rates. | Drop-out occurs if toxicity exceeds a higher threshold early. Parameters were set so Arm E had ~22.8% drop-out (modeling intolerability) and Arm H ~17%, versus Arm A ~10%. These match expectations that adding immunotherapies can slightly reduce chemo dose intensity (some patients stop early). |
| Initial tumor burden (V0) | 100 units per patient (log-normal, ~30% CV) | Set to a standardized value to represent measurable metastatic burden. In reality, patients have variable tumor loads. | We sampled V0 from a log-normal distribution (CV ~30%) so some patients start with larger tumors. Outcome metrics like ORR were calculated relative to each patient's baseline V_0. |
| RECIST response criteria | +30% shrinkage = PR; 20% growth = PD | An analysis setting: ORR was defined per RECIST (≥30% tumor volume reduction from baseline at any time for PR) and progression if ≥20% increase from nadir. | These criteria were applied to simulation outputs to compute ORR and PFS. The model's continuous tumor size outputs were thus discretized into response categories. |
