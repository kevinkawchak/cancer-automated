\uscnotepar{This document is a curated excerpt of Title 21 of the United States
Code. The chapters and sections that are not relevant to the Physical AI
oncology clinical-trial context have been removed; the retained sections are the
device-regulation backbone of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act on which
the \emph{VVUQ Physical AI Oncology Trial Bill} (H.R. 9510, Draft 1.0; prior bill
DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20454870) builds. The selection follows the legal crosswalk in
papers/VVUQ-04/instruct-bill and the statutory crosswalk in
papers/VVUQ-03/final-paper. The statutory text, headings, references, and source
credits are reproduced as they appear in the official United States Code; no
bracketed instructions or file names are inserted into the law. The purpose is to
let a future AI pass read the laws currently in place and, together with the
instruct-bill and the final paper, draft the required amendment to Public Law
119-93, Title 21.}

\uscnotepar{This excerpt is an independent research aid, not legal advice and not
an enacted law, and is not endorsed by the FDA, HHS, the Office of the Law
Revision Counsel, or any member of Congress. The authoritative text is the
United States Code as published by the Office of the Law Revision Counsel.}
