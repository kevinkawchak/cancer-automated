## output-2-update-bill

### Gemini 3.1 Pro

Drafting a bill to amend an existing law requires a specific structural approach known as **amendatory drafting**. When you are updating Public Law 119-93 (Title 21 - Food and Drugs) with new regulations for "physical AI oncology trials," you do not rewrite or reproduce the entire existing law. Instead, your bill acts as a set of instructions on how to alter the current law.

Here is a straightforward guide to answering your questions and structuring your new bill.

---

### Handling Non-Relevant Sections

The golden rule of amendatory legislative drafting is that **you only include the exact text you are changing, adding, or deleting.** * **Should non-relevant sections be removed?** You do not "remove" them; you simply **exclude** them from your draft entirely. If you aren't changing a section, it does not belong in your bill.

* **Should non-relevant headings be included in a table of contents or throughout the text?** **No.** If your new bill requires a Table of Contents (usually only necessary if the new bill is very long), it should only list the headings of your *new* bill, not the headings of the existing PL 119-93.
* **Should non-relevant sections be abbreviated?** **No.** Do not abbreviate or summarize existing law. Simply ignore any part of Title 21 that is not being directly impacted by your new AI oncology trial implementations.

---

### What the Bill Text Should Include

Your bill needs to provide precise directions to the Office of the Law Revision Counsel on where to insert your new text. It should include:

1. **The Enacting Clause:** "Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled..."
2. **Short Title:** The name of your specific new update.
3. **Amendatory Language:** Explicit instructions using terms like *strike*, *insert*, *amend*, or *add at the end*.
4. **The New Statutory Text:** The actual rules regarding the physical AI oncology trials enclosed in quotation marks.

---

### Structuring Titles, Headings, and Subheadings

Since you are not updating the entire act, the headings in your bill will represent the structure of the *amendments*, while the text inside the quotation marks will represent the structure of the *new law*.

Here is an example template of what your bill's structure and headings should look like:

**A BILL**

> To amend Public Law 119-93 to establish implementations and guidelines for physical artificial intelligence oncology trials, and for other purposes.

**SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.**

> This Act may be cited as the "**Physical AI Oncology Trials Integration Act of 2026**".

**SEC. 2. AMENDMENTS REGARDING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN ONCOLOGY TRIALS.**

> *(a) IN GENERAL.—* Title 21 of the United States Code [or Chapter X of Public Law 119-93] is amended by inserting after Section [Existing Section Number] the following new section:
> **"SEC. [New Section Number]. PHYSICAL AI ONCOLOGY TRIALS IMPLEMENTATIONS.**
> *"(a) DEFINITIONS.—* In this section...
> *"(b) TRIAL PROTOCOLS.—* The Secretary shall establish guidelines for the use of physical AI...
> *"(c) REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.—* ..."

*(Notice how the non-relevant parts of Title 21 are completely omitted. The bill only focuses on where the new "Physical AI Oncology Trials" section goes and what it says.)*
