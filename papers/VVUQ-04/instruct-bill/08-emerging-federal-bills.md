# 08 - Emerging Federal Bills (Research Influences)

Scope: introduced or in-committee bills of the 119th Congress (2025 to 2026) that
touch AI in health care. Per point c of the task, these are emerging research
influences only. They are directional signals for a memo, appendix, testimony, or
research matrix. None is enacted law, and none should appear in the operative
text of the bill.

## Role in the bill update (point c)

1. These bills do not change the bill's operative text. They populate the
   research matrix, the policy memo, and any future testimony.
2. They establish two things the bill can cite as legislative momentum: an
   appetite for autonomous clinical AI (prescribing and payment), and a
   countervailing insistence on human review of AI coverage decisions.
3. They mark the genuine statutory gap (no enacted federal human-in-the-loop or
   automated-VVUQ mandate) that file 01 identified.

## Verification cautions (do not cite as enacted or misnumber)

1. There is no verifiable diagnostics VALID Act in the 119th Congress. The bills
   titled VALID Act of 2025 (S. 1932 and H.R. 3694) are an unrelated VA-loan
   bill. Laboratory-developed-test regulation is currently governed by litigation
   and FDA rulemaking, not pending legislation. Cite the LDT status accordingly.
2. A TEST AI Act, a standalone AI in clinical trials bill, and a standalone
   federal health-AI liability bill could not be verified. Do not assign bill
   numbers to them.
3. The exact sponsor of H.Res. 694 was not cleanly confirmable; cite the bill
   number and subject, not a specific sponsor.
