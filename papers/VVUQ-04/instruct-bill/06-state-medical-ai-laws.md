# 06 - State Medical AI Laws

Scope: enacted and pending state laws that govern AI in health care and medicine.
No state law yet regulates Physical AI or robotics in clinical trials or mandates
VVUQ of interaction code, so these laws are analogues: they establish
human-in-the-loop decision-making, disclosure, bias testing, and documentation
duties that the bill can transplant and cite as mass-adopted state practice.

## Role in the bill update (points a through d)

1. Point a (currency): adds the 2024 to 2026 state enactments (California,
   Colorado, Utah, Texas, Illinois, Maryland, Nebraska, Nevada) beyond the four
   states cited in the prior bill.
2. Point b (grounding): the California Physicians Make Decisions Act and the
   Illinois WOPR Act are widely cited human-over-AI statutes.
3. Point c (influences): the comprehensive high-risk AI frameworks (Colorado,
   Texas) show the structure a Physical AI oncology regime would fit into.
4. Point d (structure): state disclosure and audit language models the bill's
   transparency and attestation clauses.

## A. California

| Key | Bill and short title | Status / effective | Substance and relevance |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| ca-ab3030 | AB 3030 (2024), health care services: artificial intelligence | Chapter 848, Statutes of 2024; effective Jan. 1, 2025 | Generative AI used for patient clinical communications must carry a disclaimer that it is AI-generated and instructions to contact a human; exempt if a licensed human reviews it. Models mandatory disclosure plus a human-contact path. |
| ca-sb1120 | SB 1120 (2024), Physicians Make Decisions Act, health care coverage utilization review | Chapter 879, Statutes of 2024; effective Jan. 1, 2025 | AI in utilization review may not replace a licensed physician's medical-necessity determination, must use the individual's clinical history, and is subject to audit. The flagship human-in-the-loop plus auditability model. |
| ca-ab489 | AB 489 (2025), health care professions: deceptive terms: artificial intelligence | Chapter 615, Statutes of 2025; operative Jan. 1, 2026 | AI may not use terms implying it holds a health-care license or is a human clinician. Models anti-impersonation, reinforcing disclosure and human oversight. |

## B. Colorado

| Key | Bill and short title | Status / effective | Substance and relevance |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| co-sb24-205 | SB 24-205, Colorado Artificial Intelligence Act | Enacted May 2024; effective June 30, 2026 (delayed by SB 25B-004); enforcement paused by a federal court Apr. 27, 2026 | Duty of care for developers and deployers of high-risk AI in consequential decisions, expressly including health-care services: reasonable care against algorithmic discrimination, risk management, impact assessments, and disclosure. The broadest risk-management and impact-assessment analogue to a VVUQ duty. |
| co-sb25b-004 | SB 25B-004 (2025 special session), implementation delay | Signed Aug. 28, 2025 | Postpones the Colorado AI Act effective date to June 30, 2026. Confirms the verified effective date for the high-risk-AI regime. |
| co-sb21-169 | SB 21-169 (2021), restrict insurers' use of external consumer data | Enacted July 6, 2021; implementing Regulation 10-1-1 effective Oct. 15, 2025 for health insurers | Requires insurers to test external data, algorithms, and predictive models for unfair discrimination and adopt a governance framework. The closest existing model to automated VVUQ plus bias auditing with documented governance. |

## C. Utah

| Key | Bill and short title | Status / effective | Substance and relevance |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| ut-sb149 | SB 149 (2024), Artificial Intelligence Policy Act | Effective May 1, 2024 (amended 2025) | Creates an AI policy office and sandbox; regulated occupations, including health professions, must conspicuously disclose generative AI before an interaction. Models up-front disclosure in licensed contexts. |
| ut-hb452 | HB 452 (2025), AI mental health chatbots | Signed Mar. 25, 2025; effective May 7, 2025 | Requires disclosure of AI use and provides an affirmative defense conditioned on documenting training data, models, and ongoing accuracy, safety, and fairness efforts. A direct cousin of a VVUQ-evidence safe harbor. |

## D. Texas

| Key | Bill and short title | Status / effective | Substance and relevance |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| tx-hb149 | HB 149 (2025), Texas Responsible Artificial Intelligence Governance Act (TRAIGA) | Signed June 22, 2025; effective Jan. 1, 2026 | Comprehensive AI governance: prohibits manipulative and discriminatory uses, creates a sandbox and an AI advisory council, and is AG-enforced. The sandbox and advisory-council structure is a venue model for piloting a Physical AI oncology regime. |
| tx-sb1822 | SB 1822 (2025), AI-based algorithms in utilization review | Passed Senate; left pending in House committee; did not pass | Would have required public disclosure of AI in utilization review and an annual attestation to the insurance department that algorithms minimize bias. A clear bias-attestation template; flagged as not enacted. |

## E. Illinois

| Key | Bill and short title | Status / effective | Substance and relevance |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| il-hb1806 | HB 1806 (2025), Wellness and Oversight for Psychological Resources (WOPR) Act | Public Act 104-0054; effective Aug. 4, 2025 | AI may not make independent therapeutic decisions or generate treatment plans without review and approval by a licensed professional. Among the strongest no-autonomous-clinical-action statutes, directly analogous to requiring licensed human oversight of a robot's patient-facing actions. |

## F. Other 2025 to 2026 enactments and guidance

| Key | State and item | Status / effective | Substance and relevance |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| md-hb820 | Maryland HB 820 (2025), utilization review: use of AI | Chapter 747; effective Oct. 1, 2025 | AI in utilization review must use individual clinical information, may not deny or modify care autonomously, and is open to audit. Reinforces human-over-AI plus auditability. |
| ne-lb77 | Nebraska LB 77 (2025), transparency in prior authorization | Approved June 4, 2025 | An AI algorithm may not be the sole basis to deny or delay care, and AI use must be disclosed. |
| nv-ab406 | Nevada AB 406 (2025), AI in mental and behavioral health | Chapter 283; effective July 1, 2025 | Bars AI from constituting the practice of professional mental health and allows AI only with independent human review of output. |
| ny-a9149 | New York A9149 (2023 to 2024), AI in utilization review | Pending; carried with companion bills | Would require submission of utilization-review algorithms to the Department of Financial Services for bias certification. Cited for the algorithm-certification model. |
| ny-dfs-cl7 | New York DFS Insurance Circular Letter No. 7 (2024), AI and external data in underwriting | In effect (guidance, July 11, 2024) | Insurers using AI must test for unfair discrimination and maintain governance and documentation. A bias-testing and governance analogue. |
| fsmb-ai | Federation of State Medical Boards, responsible and ethical incorporation of AI into clinical practice | Adopted Apr. 2024 | Augmented-intelligence framing with human accountability and continuous monitoring. The professional-norms backbone several state board-delegation statutes invoke. |

A 2026 wave continued the pattern with reported enactments in Indiana (HB 1271)
and Alabama (SB 63) requiring human review before AI-based coverage actions;
their exact public-law numbers should be confirmed on the state sites before use
in operative text.
