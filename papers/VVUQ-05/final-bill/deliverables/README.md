*Independent research draft. Not an enacted law, not pending legislation, and not
legal advice; not endorsed by the FDA, HHS, or any Member of Congress.*

This folder holds the supplementary documents a draft United States House bill
needs before it can be introduced, for **H. R. 9510**, the *Verification Before
Generation in Physical AI Oncology Trials Act of 2026* (the bill itself is the
LaTeX measure in [`..`](..), compiled separately to a PDF). Each deliverable is a
**separate, complete, submission-quality Markdown document** with
white-background tables, single hyphens, the section symbol § for codified
references, and no images or mermaid diagrams, so a sponsoring office could act on
any one of them without the others.

Two reality checks frame the package: the number **H. R. 9510 is illustrative**
(the Clerk assigns the real number only at introduction), and a private project
**cannot submit a bill directly** (only a sitting Member may introduce a House
bill). Every document is therefore aimed at getting the text into final form and
into the hands of a Member who will introduce it.

## The pre-introduction path

```
  Consolidate one authoritative text (the VVUQ-04 / v3.0 amendment)
        |
        v
  Human subject-matter and legal review  ->  currency check (deliverable 11)
        |
        v
  Route through the House Office of the Legislative Counsel (deliverable 10)
        |
        v
  Attach the Constitutional Authority Statement (06) and the PAYGO note (07)
        |
        v
  Recruit a sponsor and original cosponsors (08); brief stakeholders (09)
        |
        v
  Introduce via the eHopper -> the Clerk assigns the real H.R. number and
  the Speaker refers it to the Committee on Energy and Commerce
```

## How the package maps to the bill

- The bill is the LaTeX measure in [`papers/VVUQ-05/full-bill`](..); these
  documents accompany it and never replace its operative text.
- The findings (04), the section-by-section (02), and the comparative print (05)
  mirror SEC. 2, SEC. 3, and SEC. 4 of the bill.
- The testimony and research-influence brief (12) carries the same placements as
  Appendix D of the bill, keeping the 119th Congress emerging bills and executive
  actions out of the operative text.
