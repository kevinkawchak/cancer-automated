## Purpose and method

The draft was frozen and currency-checked "through Public Law 119-93," and the instruct-bill legal crosswalk that anchors its citations is current through May 31, 2026. This matrix performs the Phase 1 currency task that the next-steps memo identifies as a precondition to introduction: re-verify, immediately before the bill is introduced, that (1) no newer public law has changed Title 21 since Public Law 119-93; (2) the target section number 515D / 21 U.S.C. § 360e-5 is still unused; and (3) every cross-reference and each of the ten conforming amendments still lands on existing, correctly numbered law.

The method is documentary and reproducible. Each item below names the authority it depends on, the status confirmed as of June 3, 2026, and the precise action to repeat at introduction. The controlling currency sources are the Office of the Law Revision Counsel (OLRC) United States Code (uscode.house.gov), the text of Public Law 119-93, and the instruct-bill legal crosswalk current through May 31, 2026. Because the Clerk assigns the real bill number only at the moment of introduction, the illustrative "H. R. 9510" is treated throughout as a placeholder and is not itself a currency item.

This is a verification record, not an assertion that the law cannot change. Title 21 is amended frequently. The value of the matrix is that it tells a sponsoring office exactly which anchors to re-pull, and what a clean result looks like, on the day the measure is filed.

## Target-number availability (section 515D / 21 U.S.C. § 360e-5)

The Act needs one vacant section number coordinated across two systems: the FD&C Act's own section numbering and the United States Code classification. The chosen pair is FD&C Act section 515D, classified to 21 U.S.C. § 360e-5.

The number is available and well placed. The premarket-approval cluster of the FD&C Act runs section 515 (21 U.S.C. § 360e), section 515A (21 U.S.C. § 360e-1), section 515B (21 U.S.C. § 360e-2), and section 515C (21 U.S.C. § 360e-4), with the predetermined change control plan authority at section 515C / 21 U.S.C. § 360e-4. Inserting section 515D / 21 U.S.C. § 360e-5 immediately after section 515C places the new verification authority next to the change-control authority it most closely resembles, and the "-5" suffix is the next open slot in the section 360e family. As of June 3, 2026, neither section 515D nor 21 U.S.C. § 360e-5 is assigned in Title 21.

Re-verify at introduction: search the OLRC United States Code for "360e-5" and the FD&C Act table of contents for "515D"; both must return no existing section. If either has been taken by an intervening law, renumber to the next open suffix and re-run the conforming-amendment matrix, because every conforming amendment cross-references "section 515D."

## Section-number disambiguation

A recurring drafting hazard in Title 21 device law is the near-identical pair section 360(k) and section 360k. They are different provisions and must not be conflated:

- 21 U.S.C. § 360(k) is subsection (k) of section 360 (FD&C Act section 510(k)), the premarket-notification authority. The Act's section 510(k) conforming amendment, and its change-control cross-references in section 515C / 21 U.S.C. § 360e-4, rely on this provision.
- 21 U.S.C. § 360k is a separate, freestanding section (FD&C Act section 521) on State and local requirements respecting devices, that is, device preemption. The Act's State-law savings clause is the new subsection (c) of this section.

Every operative use of "510(k)" in the bill must cite 21 U.S.C. § 360(k) with the parenthetical "(k)", and every use of the preemption section must cite 21 U.S.C. § 360k without parentheses. This convention is fixed by the instruct-bill drafting checklist and must be re-checked against the frozen text before introduction.
